## 1. Purpose

Helpthread is open-source software: its core is licensed under the GNU Affero General
Public License, version 3.0 (**"AGPL-3.0"**), and everyone is free to use, modify, and
redistribute it under that license. Trademarks are different from copyright. The AGPL
governs the **code**; it does not grant rights to the project's **name and brand**. This
policy explains how you may — and may not — use the Helpthread name and marks, so that
users can always trust that something called "Helpthread" comes from, or is compatible
with, the genuine project.

The goal is narrow and traditional: keep the marks a reliable signal of origin, while
leaving the software as free as its license promises.

## 2. What this policy covers

The **"Marks"** are:

- the name **Helpthread** and any Helpthread word marks and logos;
- the Helpthread **npm organization** and its package namespace;
- the name of the official **Helpthread marketplace**.

The Marks are owned by **Resonant IQ, Inc.** (**"Resonant IQ"**). Helpthread is a
Resonant IQ product. Nothing in the AGPL-3.0, or in any Helpthread commercial module
license, transfers or licenses the Marks; they are governed by this policy and by
applicable trademark law.

## 3. Uses you may make without asking (permitted)

You do not need our permission for any of the following.

### 3.1 Nominative use — referring to Helpthread

You may use the name **Helpthread** to refer truthfully to the Helpthread project, its
software, or this organization — in documentation, articles, talks, comparisons, package
descriptions, and the like. This is ordinary **nominative fair use**: use the name only
as much as you need to identify the project, don't use our logos as if they were yours,
and don't imply that Resonant IQ sponsors or endorses you when it doesn't.

### 3.2 "Compatible with Helpthread" / "for Helpthread"

If you build a module, integration, tool, or service that works with Helpthread, you may
say so descriptively — for example **"compatible with Helpthread,"** **"for
Helpthread,"** or **"a module for Helpthread."** Use the name to describe the
relationship, not as the name of your own product, and don't format it to suggest your
product is an official Helpthread release or is endorsed by Resonant IQ.

### 3.3 Unmodified redistribution under the AGPL

If you redistribute Helpthread **unmodified**, under the AGPL-3.0, you may keep the Marks
in place — the name, logos, and notices that ship with the project. Passing along a
genuine, unmodified copy is exactly the case where the Marks should stay: what you are
distributing really is Helpthread.

## 4. Uses that require a change (forks and modified versions)

The one place the Marks and the freedom to modify meet is a **fork** — a modified version
you distribute to others. The AGPL fully protects your right to make and share
modifications. This policy asks only that a **materially modified** version not present
itself, through the Marks, as the genuine project.

Concretely: if you distribute a version of Helpthread that you have **materially
modified**, please **do not use the Helpthread name or logos in a way that implies your
version originates from, or is endorsed by, the Helpthread project or Resonant IQ.**
Give your modified version its own name and branding. This is the same courtesy the
broader free-software community has long observed — the tradition in which Firefox
becomes Iceweasel when rebuilt with changes — and it exists to protect users, not to
restrain your code: someone who downloads something called "Helpthread" should get
genuine Helpthread. You remain free, of course, to state truthfully and descriptively
that your project is **based on** or **derived from** Helpthread (per section 3.1).

We would rather ask politely than police heavily. If you're unsure whether a change is
"material," or you'd like to keep some brand continuity for a good-faith downstream
build, get in touch — see section 7.

## 5. Marketplace names

Do not use **Helpthread** — or any name confusingly similar to it or to the official
Helpthread marketplace — as the name of a competing marketplace, store, or module
directory, in a way that could lead people to believe it is the official Helpthread
marketplace or is endorsed by Resonant IQ. The official marketplace name is one of the
Marks (section 2) precisely because it is where users expect to find genuine first-party
modules; a confusingly similar name undermines that trust.

Describing your offering as **"for Helpthread"** or **"compatible with Helpthread"** is
fine (section 3.2); naming it so as to impersonate the official marketplace is not.

## 6. What we don't restrict

For the avoidance of doubt, this policy does **not** restrict:

- your rights in the **code** under the AGPL-3.0 or under any commercial module license;
- truthful, descriptive **nominative** references to Helpthread (section 3.1);
- accurate **compatibility** statements (section 3.2);
- **unmodified** AGPL redistribution with the Marks intact (section 3.3).

Trademark law already permits nominative fair use, comparative reference, and truthful
description; nothing here is meant to narrow those rights, and this policy should be read
consistently with them.
